## How she sells it (steal these)

### 1. The AICITY $197 page makes the SAME core argument as sacredlysavage's PME upsell — "the money is in what you build around it"
AICITY headline: **"You can build a hyper-realistic AI influencer. The real money is in what you build around it."** / "Here's the truth nobody in the AI space is telling you... it's not a brand." This is *identical strategic messaging* to sacredlysavage's Privé Maison Elevée ("you built the AI influencer, now build the empire"). **Two independent competitors converging on the same pitch = this is the proven high-value hook.** Strong signal for Amy's path-B product.

### 2. Social proof via testimonials + a specific result claim
AICITY page runs named testimonials ("One week in and I'm already obsessed...", "Meonah O.", "Ilana Z.") plus a hard claim: **"an AI page to 50K followers in 4 months, monetized it... students are getting results with the same system."** sacredlysavage did NOT show testimonials in captured screenshots. **Action: Amy's sales page has zero social proof yet — even one or two testimonials (or a documented "here's Amy's own numbers") would lift it.**

### 3. Explicit FAQ / objection-handling (same as sacredlysavage)
AICITY FAQ: "Is AI influencing just a trend?" / "Is this just going to teach me to sell this course?" / "What makes this different from a prompt pack?" / "Can I use this for my own brand or business?" — Note the second one: she pre-empts the cynical *"is this just a course teaching me to sell courses"* objection head-on. **Both competitors bake FAQ into the page. Amy still doesn't. This is now a confirmed pattern (n=2).**

### 4. "For you if / Not for you if" qualifier blocks
Both AICITY and AiMUSE pages use a "this is for you if… / not for you if…" section — a classic that pre-qualifies buyers and increases perceived fit. Amy could add this.

### 5. Emotional/identity framing as the differentiator
AiMUSE Alchemy sells on *feeling*: "built for the woman behind the brand," "identity over imitation," "sells with soul." She's competing on emotional resonance, not features. Different flavour from sacredlysavage's hype — but note **both avoid competing on the consistency tech itself.** They sell identity, monetisation, and belonging.

## KEY DISCOVERY: the shared "AiMUSE / AICITY" ecosystem

**mariaxelise and sacredlysavage both sell "AiMUSE Alchemy" AND "AiMUSE Seedance 2.0 Prompt Director Studio" at the same/similar prices ($147 / $67).** This is not a coincidence — it reveals one of:
- a **white-label / reseller ecosystem** (a creator built AiMUSE/AICITY and licenses or affiliates it out to multiple front-facing "AI influencers"), or
- an **80% affiliate program** (both pages mention 60–80% affiliate commissions) where these accounts resell each other's products.

Evidence: mariaxelise even resells "Bestie Thread Bank **by @Kristen_Beck**" openly. sacredlysavage's Seedance page offered "60% affiliate commissions." AiMUSE Alchemy offers "80% affiliate commission on every sale."

**This changes the competitive picture materially:** the "competitors" may be a *network of resellers* pushing a shared product suite, each fronting a different blonde AI persona, all earning affiliate commission on each other's funnels. That's a franchise-like flywheel, not isolated operators.

**Implication for Amy:** (a) the market may be more concentrated (fewer actual products, many front-ends) than it looks; (b) an **affiliate program is clearly a core growth lever** in this space — Amy has none yet; (c) Amy's differentiation matters *more*, because the personas are becoming interchangeable blonde clones selling the same underlying suite. Amy (dark-haired, operator-brained, dry, posh, boardroom-luxury) is visibly not one of the AiMUSE clones.

## Implications for Amy (the counter-positioning)

1. **Confirmed pattern (n=2): the durable/high-value product is "now monetise your character as a real multi-stream brand," NOT the consistency method.** Both top-enders ($197 AICITY, $57 PME) sell this. Amy's path-B product is validated twice over. Price it in the $57–$197 band.
2. **Amy needs three things both competitors have and she lacks:** (a) an **FAQ/objection block** on the sales page, (b) **social proof / a result claim**, (c) an **affiliate program** (the space runs on 60–80% commissions).
3. **The lane is saturating with interchangeable blonde AiMUSE-clones.** Amy's visual + tonal differentiation (dark, dry, posh, operator) is now a *bigger* asset than at n=1 — the sea of sameness makes a distinct persona stand out more, not less.
4. **"Trial Reels Done Right" ($37) is a validated micro-product** — a pure IG-growth-strategy product separate from the character method. Amy's content skill could be productised the same way (a "how Amy's clips are made / how to get reach" micro-product).
