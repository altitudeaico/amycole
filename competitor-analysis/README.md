# Competitor Analysis — Amy Cole / The Phantom Method

## What this is

A living intelligence file on the businesses operating in Amy Cole's space: **meta-model AI influencers** — accounts that build a consistent AI persona and then monetise mainly by *selling the method* to others who want to do the same, rather than (or alongside) brand deals.

We study them to answer three questions:
1. **Does the model work?** (validation — who's making money, at what scale)
2. **What can we steal?** (product ladder, pricing, sales-page structure, messaging, funnel mechanics)
3. **Where's our wedge?** (how Amy is positioned *differently* so she isn't competing head-on)

This is not idle research. Every finding should convert to a decision: a product to build, a price to set, a page to rewrite, or a positioning to sharpen.

## Cross-competitor patterns (updated as we add more)

*n=3. Patterns confirmed across accounts are marked [CONFIRMED]; the network structure is now mapped.*

1. **[CONFIRMED n=3] The money is in the ladder, not the single product.** Everyone runs a laddered offer (free → $197+). Our £24 Phantom is one under-priced rung. The proven entry-course price is the **$197 band**, not £24.
2. **[CONFIRMED n=3] Objection-handling / FAQ + social proof belong on the sales page.** All show FAQs; the owner (zuri) also shows testimonials AND inbound-brand-DM screenshots. Amy's page has neither. **Highest-priority page fixes: add FAQ + social proof.**
3. **[CONFIRMED n=3] The durable, premium product is "now build a REAL multi-stream BRAND," not "keep a face consistent."** The sharpest version is zuri's origin copy: *"That's not a brand. That's a funnel. What if your AI influencer was an actual brand?"* The consistency method is commoditised; the branded business is the premium sell. **This is Amy's validated path-B product — adopt this frame.**
4. **[CONFIRMED n=5 — the biggest finding] A DISTINCTIVE persona LEADS every lane; blonde clones are the middle.** Five accounts, five distinct archetypes, zero overlap: blonde-luxury (sacredlysavage, mariaxelise), editorial-fashion (zuri), manosphere-uncle (uncgotgame), faith-feminine (sophiareviera). The two biggest (uncgotgame 273K, zuri 100K) are the most distinctive; the blonde clones are smaller and mostly resell zuri's product. **The business/operator/founder-wisdom lane is genuinely UNCONTESTED — no one is doing dry, sharp, boardroom-coded wisdom. That lane is Amy's. Distinctiveness is the growth strategy, and Amy's is leader-shaped AND in open water.**
5. **[UPGRADED n=5] There are THREE viable business shapes — pick deliberately:**
   - **Deep ladder × mid audience, sell the METHOD** (AiMUSE pyramid: free → $197 + B2B/1:1/affiliate; 36–100K followers).
   - **Single product × huge audience, sell the METHOD** (uncgotgame: one ~$75 PDF, 273K followers).
   - **Persona → sell a REAL product/service to normal consumers** (sophiareviera: AI persona fronts an owned pet-portrait e-com brand). **Least saturated, arguably most durable — the creator-course market is finite and incestuous; the consumer/brand market is not.**
   Amy is currently building shape #1 (Phantom = sell the method) — the *most* saturated. Shape #3 (+ the B2B agency zuri hints at) is the blue water. **Strong signal: Amy's persona may be worth more pointed at selling a real service (B2B AI-content agency) than at selling "the method" to other creators.**
6. **[NEW n=5] Amy's FORMAT is validated with hard numbers.** sophiareviera runs almost exactly Amy's format (seated AI woman, podcast mic, warm set, one-line wisdom) and pulls 6K–165K views per reel on only 6.9K followers. **The content engine is de-risked; the format demonstrably travels.**
7. **[NEW n=5] Track VIEWS, not followers.** sophiareviera at 6.9K followers / 165K-view reels shows follower count is a vanity metric here. Assess Amy's traction on views + saves.
8. **[CONFIRMED] Shared "AiMUSE/AICITY" ecosystem = a pyramid.** @zuri_ladiva OWNS AICITY ($197); front-accounts resell on 60–80% commission. Real money accrues to the owner. **An affiliate program is a growth lever Amy lacks.**
9. **[the whitespace, sharper still] B2B + "sell a real thing" is the blue ocean.** zuri touches B2B (sell AI campaigns to brands); sophiareviera sells a real consumer product. Both point away from the saturated "sell the method to creators" game. uncgotgame's provocation lane actively *blocks* B2B (not brand-safe). Amy's brand-safe operator persona is uniquely suited to B2B/real-product plays the bikini-blondes and provocation-uncle can't credibly do.
10. **[CONFIRMED] Pricing + page fixes for shape #1:** £24 is fine vs uncgotgame (~£75 for a 10-page PDF) but low vs the AiMUSE $197 entry course — reconsider Phantom price/ladder position. And add FAQ/objection-handling + social proof (testimonials, inbound-brand-DMs) — every ladder player has them, Amy has neither.
11. **[NEW n=6] The distinctiveness thesis is so real that a competitor sells it as her literal hook.** tamala's whole pitch is "I don't want to blend in / content that stands out." Every AI creator fears being a clone (and most are). **Amy can out-own this because she EMBODIES it (operator persona in a blonde-clone market), not just claims it. "Everyone else built the same girl" is a hook Amy is uniquely entitled to.**
12. **[NEW n=6] Free lead magnets should be engineered to SPREAD, not just gate an email.** tamala's "STEAL MY PROMPTS" (Canva) is generous, screenshot-native, and asks for a tag — it markets itself and seeds the paid products. Amy's "Drift Problem" is a gated PDF. **Action: add a shareable/taggable version of Amy's free magnet (a carousel or graphic that travels on its own).**
13. **[CALIBRATION] Weight findings by proven-at-scale vs early-stage. Verification is buyable (not a success signal).** The proven players are uncgotgame (273K) and zuri (100K). sophiareviera (6.9K) and tamala (1.2K) are early — instructive on tactics/format but NOT proof of a working business. Several tiny accounts are blue-check verified (Meta Verified is paid), so ignore the blue check as a proxy for success.

**The one-line strategic takeaway:** Distinctiveness is the engine and Amy's operator lane is uncontested; her seated-wisdom format is proven to travel (sophiareviera). But she's building in the MOST saturated business shape (sell-the-method). The three biggest unlocks: (1) claim the open operator-wisdom content lane and own the "everyone else built the same girl" hook, (2) shift monetisation toward the blue ocean — B2B AI-content agency or a real productised offering (shape #3), not another creator course, (3) if continuing the Phantom course, add FAQ + social proof, reconsider pricing/ladder, and make the free magnet shareable. Judge traction by VIEWS, not followers — and weight competitor lessons by whether they come from a proven-at-scale account.

4. Add a row to the **Competitors tracked** table above.
5. Update **Cross-competitor patterns** if the new account confirms or breaks a hypothesis.
