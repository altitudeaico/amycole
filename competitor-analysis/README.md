# Competitor Analysis — Amy Cole / The Phantom Method

## What this is

A living intelligence file on the businesses operating in Amy Cole's space: **meta-model AI influencers** — accounts that build a consistent AI persona and then monetise mainly by *selling the method* to others who want to do the same, rather than (or alongside) brand deals.

We study them to answer three questions:
1. **Does the model work?** (validation — who's making money, at what scale)
2. **What can we steal?** (product ladder, pricing, sales-page structure, messaging, funnel mechanics)
3. **Where's our wedge?** (how Amy is positioned *differently* so she isn't competing head-on)

This is not idle research. Every finding should convert to a decision: a product to build, a price to set, a page to rewrite, or a positioning to sharpen.

**To go deeper on any competitor, open their folder.** The README stays high-level on purpose.

## Cross-competitor patterns (updated as we add more)

*n=3. Patterns confirmed across accounts are marked [CONFIRMED]; the network structure is now mapped.*

1. **[CONFIRMED n=3] The money is in the ladder, not the single product.** Everyone runs a laddered offer (free → $197+). Our £24 Phantom is one under-priced rung. The proven entry-course price is the **$197 band**, not £24.
2. **[CONFIRMED n=3] Objection-handling / FAQ + social proof belong on the sales page.** All show FAQs; the owner (zuri) also shows testimonials AND inbound-brand-DM screenshots. Amy's page has neither. **Highest-priority page fixes: add FAQ + social proof.**
3. **[CONFIRMED n=3] The durable, premium product is "now build a REAL multi-stream BRAND," not "keep a face consistent."** The sharpest version is zuri's origin copy: *"That's not a brand. That's a funnel. What if your AI influencer was an actual brand?"* The consistency method is commoditised; the branded business is the premium sell. **This is Amy's validated path-B product — adopt this frame.**
4. **[CONFIRMED n=4 — the biggest finding] A DISTINCTIVE persona LEADS the market; blonde clones are the middle.** The two largest accounts are both maximally distinctive and neither is a blonde clean-girl: @uncgotgame_ (273K, older Black "AI Uncle," manosphere-comedy) and @zuri_ladiva (100K, editorial-fashion Black woman). The interchangeable blondes (sacredlysavage 45K, mariaxelise 36.5K) sit *below* them and largely resell zuri's product. **Distinctiveness is the growth strategy, not a risk. Amy's differentiation (operator-brain, dry, posh, boardroom-luxury) is leader-shaped. This is now heavily de-risked (n=4).**
5. **[NEW n=4] There are TWO viable business shapes, pick deliberately:**
   - **Deep ladder × mid audience** (AiMUSE pyramid: free → $197, plus B2B/1:1/affiliate; 36–100K followers). Monetises *depth per follower*.
   - **Single product × huge audience** (uncgotgame: one ~$75 PDF, 273K followers). Monetises *reach*.
   Both work. Ladder is the safer, more controllable build; reach is higher-ceiling but depends on genuinely viral distinctive content. **Amy should hedge: build a small ladder (control) while swinging for reach (upside).**
6. **[CONFIRMED] Shared "AiMUSE / AICITY" ecosystem = a pyramid.** @zuri_ladiva OWNS AICITY ($197); front-accounts resell on 60–80% commission. Real money accrues to the owner. **An affiliate program is a core growth lever Amy lacks.**
7. **[NEW — the whitespace, and now sharper] B2B is nearly untouched, and Amy is uniquely suited to it.** Only zuri touches B2B (selling AI campaigns to brands). uncgotgame's provocation lane actively *blocks* B2B (not brand-safe). Amy's brand-safe operator-luxury persona is the ideal B2B trust asset. **Amy's strongest long-term wedge: run the AI-content agency for brands, not sell the method to creators.**
8. **[NEW n=4] Pricing: £24 is fine vs uncgotgame (~£75 for a 10-page PDF) but low vs the AiMUSE $197 entry course.** Amy's image-rich Phantom PDF could price at $47–75, OR become the entry/free rung under a $197 "build & monetise" product. **Action: reconsider Phantom pricing / ladder position.**
9. **[CONFIRMED] Objection-handling/FAQ + social proof belong on the sales page.** All the ladder players do it; the owner adds testimonials + inbound-brand-DM screenshots. Amy has neither yet. **Highest-priority page fixes.**

**The one-line strategic takeaway:** The meta model is persona-agnostic and supports two shapes (deep-ladder or reach-plus-one-product). A DISTINCTIVE persona leads every lane — the blonde AiMUSE resellers are the middle, not the top, and are mostly distribution for one owner's product. Amy's differentiation is leader-shaped. The bluest water is **B2B brand partnerships**, where Amy's brand-safe operator persona is a trust asset the provocation-accounts and bikini-blondes can't match.

## How to add a new competitor

1. Create `competitor-analysis/[handle]/` with a `screenshots/` subfolder.
2. Drop source screenshots in, renamed by content (e.g. `03-profile-links-bio.jpg`).
3. Write `analysis.md` using the standard structure (see the `competitor-analysis` skill, or copy sacredlysavage's as a template): Snapshot → Character/vibe → What they sell → How they sell it → Implications for Amy → Open threads.
4. Add a row to the **Competitors tracked** table above.
5. Update **Cross-competitor patterns** if the new account confirms or breaks a hypothesis.
