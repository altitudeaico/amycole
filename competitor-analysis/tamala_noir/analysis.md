**Archetype #6, still distinct:** none of the others own the hyperpop-maximalist-aesthetic lane. Confirms again — every viable account picks a distinct lane. Hers is "cinematic AI artistry."

## How she sells it (steal these)

### 1. "STEAL MY PROMPTS" as a viral free lead magnet — the mechanic in full (screenshot 03)
A Canva-hosted free prompt sheet. The genius of the mechanic: the post itself ("STEAL MY PROMPTS!") is a scroll-stopping, generous-feeling hook that spreads, and the free sheet includes instructions that quietly teach *her* method + point back to her: **"Insert your avatar as reference photos to keep consistency"** (the locked-reference method again) and **"Enjoy creating.. & please tag me!"** (built-in attribution/reach loop). **Free value that markets itself and seeds the paid products.** Amy's free "Drift Problem" is the equivalent — but Amy's isn't engineered to be *shared* and *tagged* the way "STEAL MY PROMPTS" is. **Action: make Amy's free magnet a shareable, taggable, screenshot-native asset, not just a gated PDF.**

### 2. Positioning built on ONE line: "I don't want to blend in"
It's a story-highlight, a feed post, and the spine of her bio ("content that stands out," "stop blending in"). **This is literally Amy's differentiation thesis, verbalised as a hook.** It resonates because every AI creator secretly fears being a clone (and most are). **Amy can own this even harder — Amy IS the anti-clone (dark, dry, operator, in a sea of blondes). "Everyone else built the same girl" is a hook Amy is uniquely entitled to.**

### 3. Aggressive discount anchoring
Every product shows was/now (e.g. $157→$65, 59% off). Standard but consistent. Creates urgency + value perception on cheap products.

### 4. Naming as product-ising
"The Feed Cohesion Engine," "Project: Hyper Real," "The Final Prompt" — evocative, benefit/mystery names (Myron-style) rather than "prompt pack #3." Good naming discipline on small products.

## Implications for Amy

1. **She confirms the distinctiveness thesis by SAYING it: "I don't want to blend in" / "content that stands out" is her entire pitch.** This validates Amy's positioning from yet another angle (n=6) — and hands Amy a sharper hook. Amy isn't just *saying* "stand out"; Amy visibly IS the outlier (operator persona in a blonde-clone market). **Amy can out-execute this exact positioning because Amy embodies it, not just claims it.**

2. **The free lead magnet should be engineered to SPREAD, not just to gate an email.** "STEAL MY PROMPTS" works because it's generous, screenshot-native, and asks for a tag. Amy's "Drift Problem" PDF is currently a gated download. **Add a shareable, taggable version** (a carousel or single graphic of "5 prompts" that travels on its own and points back to Amy).

3. **Low-priced digital ladder ($17–65) is a validated entry band** — but note she has NO high tier, NO B2B, NO real-product play. She's purely in the saturated shape #1, at the small end. She's a *cautionary* data point as much as an instructive one: sophisticated branding + tiny audience (1.2K) = not yet a proven business. **Branding without reach doesn't pay. Reach (views) is still the gate.**

4. **Feed cohesion as a product idea** — "The Feed Cohesion Engine" ($17.50) is a neat micro-product: helping people make their AI feed look consistent/branded. Adjacent to Amy's consistency method. A possible cheap Amy micro-product, though it's shape #1 (saturated).
